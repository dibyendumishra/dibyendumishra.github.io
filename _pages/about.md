I am a **researcher and data science practitioner** who is interested in studying power structures in society and using data science and interpretive methods for helping make them more equitable. 

I am currently a **Data Scientist** with the **[Ministry of Rural Development (Govt. of India)](https://rural.nic.in/en)** where I work as a member of its Data and Insights Unit. My work focuses on studying, designing, and building data-driven algorithmic solutions (mostly hybrid human-AI systems) using participatory methods towards **fair and equitable distribution** of resources and services amongst the most underserved populations in India living in rural areas and helping improve the state’s capacity through **decision support systems**. A large part of my work also involves thinking about the political economy in which such technology operates, which I am always excited to talk about!

In the past, I've worked at **[Microsoft Research India](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/lab/microsoft-research-india/)** under [Prof. Joyojeet Pal](http://joyojeet.people.si.umich.edu/) on projects around **political polarisation, crisis informatics, and disinformation** on social media using computational methods. The focus of these projects revolved mostly around the political self of the understudied non-political class of actors, broadly categorized as influencers, who yield immense influence due to their following and reach on these platforms. The work has been shared through publications (at CSCW, COMPASS, ICWSM, SMSociety, HICCS, etc), media articles, and blog posts.
