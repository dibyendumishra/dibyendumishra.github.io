I am a **data scientist and researcher** who works broadly in the area of Information & Communication Technologies and Development(ICTD), motivated by issues of social justice. My fields of inquiry thus far have been in public sector algorithms and social networks.

At present, I am a Data Scientist with the **[Ministry of Rural Development (Govt. of India)](https://rural.nic.in/en)** which caters to the most underserved populations living in rural India(estimated at 833M as per Census 2011). As part of my work, I study, design and build meaningful in-situ algorithmic systems using participatory methods for improving service delivery. Specifically, I identify bias in resource allocation, exclusion of marginalized populations, and anomalous behavior in service delivery, and build decision aid systems for administrators for fairer and inclusive outcomes. A large part of my work also involves thinking about the political economy in which such technology operates and making them more explainable, which I am always excited to talk about!

My previous work at **[Microsoft Research India](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/lab/microsoft-research-india/)** with **[Prof. Joyojeet Pal](http://joyojeet.people.si.umich.edu/)** focused on political polarisation, crisis informatics, and disinformation on social media using computational methods. We primarily explored the political self of an understudied class of 'non-political' actors, generally known as influencers, whose following and reach on these platforms make them very influential. The work has been shared through publications (at CSCW, COMPASS, ICWSM, SMSociety, HICCS, etc), media articles, and blog posts.
